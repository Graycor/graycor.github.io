Welcome

This page exists as a personal reference — a working map of what a carnivore diet actually covers, and why I believe it covers it well. The premise is straightforward: with a varied selection of meats, eggs, dairy, and fish, the full spectrum of human nutritional requirements can be met — often exceeded — without relying on plant sources.

The table below charts each nutrient alongside notes on its role and relevance within a carnivore framework. Some entries include caveats worth understanding — such as reduced Vitamin C requirements in the absence of dietary glucose, or the nuanced case for organ meats as nature's multivitamin. These are not fringe ideas; they are grounded in biochemistry and increasingly supported by emerging research.

This is a living document. It reflects my current understanding and will be updated as my thinking evolves. For the full breakdown — including RDIs, quantities, and specific food sources — see the Deep Dive tab.
